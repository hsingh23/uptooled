Warm-Up & Cool-Down

Your warm-up is arguably the most important part of your day for protecting your body’s longevity. Do not skip your cool-down.

The Ideal Warm-Up (Prehab)

  • ✓
    Duration: It should not take more than 30 minutes.
  • ✓
    Mobilize & Stabilize: Include your PT exercises and work evenly between sides.
  • ✓
    Core & Resistance: Abdominal work and Thera-Bands for upper body/back support stability and posture.
  • ✓
    Key Exercises: Toe Taps, Bridges, Clam Shells, Side Steps/Monster Walks, Single Leg RDLs.
  • ✓
    Elevate Heart Rate: Use HIIT-style movements to get your heart rate up so you don't start cold. Fatigue leads to mistakes.
  • ✓
    Focus on Stabilizers: Prioritize strengthening smaller stabilizer muscles (rotators, inner thighs) as larger ones overfire with fatigue.

Effective Cool-Down

  • ✓
    Roll Out: Use a foam roller on quads, glutes, psoas, QL/low back, and hamstrings.
  • ✓
    Stretch: Perform static stretches on key muscle groups to improve flexibility and reduce tightness.
  • ✓
    Ice & Heat: Use ice for inflammation after dancing and heat before to loosen muscles.
  • ✓
    Anti-Inflammatories: When needed, consider topical solutions like Voltaren, CBD, Tiger Balm, or Arnica.

Note on Cardio

Cardio like running or cycling tightens muscles (hip flexors, hamstrings). Always stretch/cool down thoroughly afterward. Differentiate between warm-up cardio and a full workout training session.

The Power of Cross-Training

Dancers are athletes who train intensely year-round. Cross-training is a first line of defense against injuries caused by repetition and asymmetry in choreography.

✓ Reduces risk of overuse injuries

✓ Builds strength in underused muscles

✓ Boosts endurance and overall fitness

✓ Helps prevent burnout by adding variety

Pilates, Yoga, Gyrotonic

For core strength, flexibility, and alignment.

Somatic Training

Calm the nervous system and rework neuromuscular body patterning.

Strength Training

Build muscle endurance and power.

Cardio

Running, cycling, or swimming to improve stamina.

Mental Health is Health.

Dance is deeply emotional and demanding. Your thoughts, emotions, and stress levels directly affect how your body performs. You are a person first.

Healthy Habits to Practice:

  • Mindful movement before class: breathing, visualizing alignment.
  • Journaling about what went well, not only what to fix.
  • Talking with mentors, friends, or therapists who understand performance pressure.
  • Setting boundaries with rehearsal hours or social media.
  • Taking rest seriously—physical and mental breaks allow artistry to flourish.
  • Balancing work and play to cultivate a full life outside your craft.
